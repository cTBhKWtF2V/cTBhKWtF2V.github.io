  • Lund University seeks to be a world-class university that works to understand, explain and improve our world and the human condition.
    Lund is ranked as one of the top 100 universities in the world. The university provides education and research in engineering, science, law, social sciences, economics and management, medicine, humanities, theology, fine art, music and drama.
    Over 47,700 students and 7,500 employees are based at campuses in Lund, Malmo and Helsingborg.

  • Lund is a safe city with the youngest population in the country. The students at Lund University comprise a large part of the population of the city and contribute to its youthful, laid-back atmosphere.
    Lund is one of the oldest cities in Sweden dating back to 990. This makes it a city of contrasts where 1,000 years of history blend with modern knowledge and ideas. In Lund, you will find the charm of a small picturesque, cobble-stoned city, side-by-side with big city attractions such as shopping, restaurants and cultural events.

  • Lund University is truly international. The university cooperates with 680 partner universities in over 50 countries and is the only Swedish university to be a member of strong international networks LERU (the League of European Research Universities) and Universitas 21.

Courses

The Department of Industrial Design and the School of Architecture demand 3 years of prior studies in the subject area as well as a portfolio.
There is a high demand for courses at the School of Economics and Management. Therefore, Lund cannot guarantee a full-time course load of economics, management and business related coursework.
Due to a limited number of spaces, the Faculty of Performing Arts, the Malmo Art Academy and the Faculty of Medicine admit mainly students within specific faculty/department agreements.
For the Faculty of Performing Arts and the Malmo Art Academy, students must send a portfolio and have completed at least one full year of studies in the discipline.
Journalism courses are taught in Swedish, and are generally not available to exchange students.
Please note that certain courses listed on Lund’s website are available only to students registered for a full degree program and, as such, are not open to exchange students.

Living

Accommodation

Students can apply for housing via a separate application.
Application dates will be provided at the link above. There is no housing guarantee for exchange students; therefore you are encouraged to apply as soon as the application opens.

Health insurance

Sweden has a reciprocal health care agreement with Australia.
Note: Please be aware that travel insurance is a compulsory requirement of an exchange program.

Key information

Visa

You must apply for a “Residence Permit for a visiting student” through the Swedish Embassy or Consulate in your home country before you travel to Sweden. This procedure can take 2 to 3 months so you are advised to start your application as early as possible.
Please note that visa requirements are subject to change. Please seek advice from the Swedish consulate for the most updated information.
