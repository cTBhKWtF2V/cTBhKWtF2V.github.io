You will learn in detail about these challenges from local experts in the research, government and NGO sectors in China. Major issues studied on the tour include managing water scarcity under pressure of urbanisation and climate change; protecting ground and surface water resources from pollution and depletion; monitoring and reducing air pollution in some of the worst affected cities on earth, and building sustainable infrastructure in growing mega-cities. Students will develop their own innovative solutions to deal with a selected environmental challenge and present this at a mini-conference.

Cost

Students organise and pay for their own airfares, travel insurance, visas and meals. Accommodation will be arranged by RMIT and will cost between $80 and $100 per night. Internal travel in China will be arranged by RMIT, totalling approximately $250. The total estimated cost of the tour (inclusive) per student is approximately $3000

N.B. This does not include tuition.

Funding

The first ten students to apply and be accepted into the program will receive a $2,000 AsiaBound grant.
There are many other ways to fund your trip. Please check the Education Abroad website for funding options.
